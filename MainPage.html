Surrounded by the Indian Ocean Sri Lanka’s coast is lined with palm-lined golden beaches and the island is full of romantic landscapes, wildlife-rich jungles stirring mountains, lush green tea gardens with occasional waterfalls.

The culture triangle in the center of Sri Lanka holds a certain magic with ruins of ancient kingdoms, tanks, hidden rock caves, majestic tanks, sacred temples revealing the fascinating and almost unbelievable ancient art and architecture.

The north of Sri Lanka drum to a beat of their own, with a unique flavor and culture. Jaffna is rebuilding and healing bearing the scars of civil war and yet holds an incredible diversity influenced by Portuguese, Dutch, Tamil, Muslim and British characters.

People and Life in Sri Lanka

Daily life in Sri Lanka is intoxicating. There’s always something going on in Sri Lanka. The colourful streets are abuzz 24 hours a day, from vendors selling ripe king coconuts to tuk tuk drivers beeping their way through traffic. Conversations on street corners are so common as if everyone knows each other in Sri Lanka.

Ask anyone who has visited Sri Lanka and they’ll agree that Sri Lankans are the friendliest people in the world. From welcoming smiles and head bobbles to the generous and welcoming nature, people are genuinely warm and just downright nice.
