Interior Landscape

Deep inside the island’s central highlands, impressive paddy terraces, misty hills, picturesque waterfalls, tea gardens, lush green jungles can be found. Sri Lanka has all kinds of waterfalls fed by two half-yearly monsoons and some which are seasonal.

Most waterfalls are inaccessible and requires a few hours of hiking to reach which makes them more magical.

The water is falling with full force from an altitude that gives you goose bumps surrounded by lush green forests and rocky terrains.

The tallest Bambarakanda Falls

Bambarakanda falls is ranked 299th amongst the tallest water falls in the world being 263 meters high. It is situated in the steep pine forested slopes of Badulla district with a picturesque view from top of the mountain.

The Widest Bomburu Falls

Located bordering Nuwara Eliya and Badulla districts, Bomburu falls is the widest and one of the most scenic waterfalls in Sri Lanka. It is a seasonal waterfall and sometimes dries up during the dry season.

Bakers Falls

Bakers falls cascades down from the Horton Plains near World’s end and is named after the famous British explorer, Sir Samuel Bakers. Fed from Belihul Oya it forms many streams as it falls down.

Bopath Falls

Taking the shape of a ‘Bo’ leaf, Bopath Ella falls through two narrow rocks and then widens into a breath-taking cascade. Located in Devipahala close to Ratnapura, this Water fall tempts travelers passing by to visit and stay.
